Turkish Pronunciation Guide:
ş = "sh" as in "shoe"
ç = "ch" as in "chair"
ğ = silent, lengthens the preceding vowel
ı (undotted i) = like "u" in "radium" - a back, unrounded vowel
i (dotted) = "ee" as in "see"
ö = like German "ö" or French "eu"
ü = like German "ü" or French "u"

Cultural Note - Respect: Turkish culture highly values respect (saygı). Always address older people and officials formally. When speaking to parents, use "Siz" (formal you) rather than "Sen" (informal). When speaking to players' fathers, "Bey" after first name shows respect (e.g., "Ahmet Bey").

Important! Turkish has some unique sounds and grammar patterns. Here are common mistakes to avoid:

Key Cultural Tips for Coaching in Turkey:
  • Hospitality (misafirperverlik) - Accept tea and food when offered; refusing can offend
  • Respect (saygı) - Use formal language with elders and officials
  • "Hocam" - Players will call you this (means "my teacher/coach") - it's respectful
  • Football passion - Turkish football is intense; embrace the energy
  • Stay neutral on clubs - Don't pick sides in Galatasaray/Fenerbahçe/Beşiktaş rivalry
  • Physical contact - Handshakes and shoulder pats are normal; hugging is okay for celebrations
  • Punctuality - Be on time; Turkish professionals expect punctuality
  • Family involvement - Family is central; be welcoming to parents
